# english-plus

## 1. No silent letters

Spell every sound you pronounce and nothing more.

### Examples:

Teroodaktool (pterodactyl),  
Niif (knife).


## 2. Double letter pronounced as the letter’s name

### Examples:

Waaneeng (waning),  
Aar (air),  
Aat (ate),  
Meet (meet),  
See (see),  
Biit (bite),  
Boot (boat),  
Dotee (duty).


## 3. Ph → F

### Examples:

Fone (phone),  
Lleefent (elephant).


## 4. Th → D or F (based on pronunciation)

### Examples:

Fin (thin),  
Fot (thought),  
Dis (this),  
Den (then),  
Deh (the).  


## 5. C → K or S (based on pronunciation)

### Examples:

Kat (cat),  
Khaar (chair),  
Sent (cent).


## 6. X → Ks or Gz (based on pronunciation)

### Examples:

Foks (fox),  
Egzakt (exact).



## 7. I → E or Ahy (based on pronunciation)

### Examples:

Ii (I),  
Eglo (Igloo).


## 8. Ed → T (past tense)

### Examples:

Walkt (walked),  
Jumpt (jumped).


## 9. Q → Kua

### Examples:

Kuaency (Quincy),  
Kualefii (Qualify).


## 10. Every word is spelt exactly how its pronounced.

### Examples:

Original:

`The quick brown fox jumped over the lazy dog.`

English+

`Deh kwek brawn foks jumpt oovr deh laazee dag.`
